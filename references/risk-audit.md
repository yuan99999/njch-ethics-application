# Risk Audit

Use this before submission or when the user asks “还有什么问题”.

## Missing Items

- Version number/date absent or inconsistent across documents.
- No clear study period, sample size basis, or inclusion/exclusion criteria.
- Routine-care data and research-only procedures are not separated.
- Waiver request lacks minimal-risk, impracticability, rights/welfare, and privacy arguments.
- Privacy section does not state de-identification, access control, storage, retention, and aggregate reporting.
- Pediatric protection is not addressed for child participants.
- Consent is written only for adults while the study enrolls minors or otherwise needs guardian/assent handling.
- Biospecimen source, storage, secondary use, export/sharing, volume, or disposal is unclear when samples are involved.
- Research team table lacks role/task, phone/email, training status, or signature reminder.

## Ethics Risks

- Writing suggests intervention, prospective contact, extra testing, or treatment change when the study is actually retrospective.
- Consent is waived but the protocol describes direct contact, follow-up, questionnaires, or new sample collection.
- Data fields include rare disease, images, genetic data, or highly identifiable information without stronger privacy controls.
- Procedures exceed minimal risk without direct benefit, or pain/distress reduction and stopping rules are missing for children.
- Recruitment through treating clinicians may pressure families or staff; voluntariness and non-coercion wording is weak.
- Drug/device/IVD submissions mix sponsor documents, investigational product/device documents, investigator qualifications, insurance/compensation, and hospital forms instead of listing them separately.
- SAE, deviation, amendment, follow-up, suspension/termination, or close-out reports use the wrong event-specific form.
- Sample size wording conflicts with final analyzable cohort.
- The protocol overstates clinical benefit to prior participants in a retrospective study.
- Internal data cleaning decisions are described in a way that may distract reviewers or raise avoidable reliability concerns.

## Safer Wording

- Replace causal claims with association language.
- Replace “直接获益” with “通常无直接个人获益” for retrospective data-only studies.
- Replace “招募” with “拟调取既往病例资料”.
- Replace “研究开始时间” with “伦理委员会批准后” when work should start after approval.

## Questions For PI

- Which department/unit should appear on the form and researcher table?
- Is there external funding, sponsor, third-party cooperation, or conflict of interest?
- Are any biospecimens, images, genetic data, or exported data involved?
- Does the ethics office require PI CV/GCP certificate/dept approval upload?
- Who will manually sign the waiver/protocol commitment sections?
